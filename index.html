The Pattern

Five competitors, five different category claims, not one calls itself a transparent two-sided marketplace. Section 4 — The April Dunford Workup

Why Transparent B2B Demand Marketplace wins.

Six positioning components from April Dunford’s methodology, applied to the proposed winning framing. Sources for every claim are linked in Section 5.

01 — Competitive Alternatives

What the buyer compares Audyence to.

  • • Broker-model managed lead-gen vendors (Madison Logic, NetLine, INFUSE, DemandScience, Anteriad)
  • • Pipeline360 (managed-service marketplace; rebranded from Integrate, Aug 2025)
  • • ABM platforms / wallet-share competitors (6sense, Demandbase, Channel99)
  • • Adjacent budget categories (LinkedIn Ads, paid search, owned content)
02 — Unique Attributes

What Audyence has that the alternatives don’t.

  • • Two-sided platform architecture — buyer-side platform plus seller-side publisher sign-up
  • • Publisher-level reporting exposed to the advertiser (Reporting for Advertisers, May 7, 2026)
  • • No platform fees on top of media — cost-side advantage vs broker markup
  • • Real-time, self-serve campaign operation — buyers run campaigns end-to-end
03 — Value

What those attributes enable for the buyer.

  • • Transparency. Publisher-level performance visibility, not aggregated black-box reporting.
  • • Velocity. Self-serve operation cuts time-to-market materially — Karl’s framing: 95% faster vs traditional content syndication.
  • • Better economics. No platform fee plus premium publisher network plus outcomes-based pricing lowers cost per lead.
04 — Best-Fit Customer

Who Audyence is built for.

Large enterprise B2B advertisers running content syndication and CPL programs at scale, with in-house marketing operations to operate a real-time platform directly — not hand off to a managed service.

Six personas per the candidate brief: Demand Gen Director, Marketing Ops Lead, CMO, Performance Marketing Lead, ABM Director, RevOps Director.

Existing logos that match: Veeam, Inovalon, BitSight, Walgreens, CloudFlare, Lumen, ServiceNow, 1Password, Cohere, Mitel, Square, Tubi.

05 — Market Category

The lane.

A two-sided programmatic marketplace, sitting next to the broker-model content syndication category — not inside it. The “Transparent” qualifier defines the lane against incumbents whose business model is opacity (margin stack hidden behind a managed service).

06 — Trends That Make It Timely

Why now.

  • • Answer Engine Optimization (AEO). 94% of B2B buyers using genAI in their buying process (Forrester 2025). Only two-sided marketplaces can natively measure publisher-side AI signals.
  • • Broker fatigue. Buyers want supply-path visibility, not aggregated CPL bidding. FTC 2025 lead-gen enforcement adds regulatory tailwind.
  • • Publisher consolidation. Informa/TechTarget merger, Microsoft licensing deals — premium publishers want direct platform relationships.
